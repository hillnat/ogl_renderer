{\footnotesize\ttfamily \#define AI\+\_\+\+SCENE\+\_\+\+FLAGS\+\_\+\+TERRAIN~0x10}

Denotes pure height-\/map terrain data. Pure terrains usually consist of quads, sometimes triangles, in a regular grid. The x,y coordinates of all vertex positions refer to the x,y coordinates on the terrain height map, the z-\/axis stores the elevation at a specific point.

TER (Terragen) and HMP (3D Game Studio) are height map formats. \begin{DoxyNote}{Note}
\mbox{\hyperlink{namespace_assimp}{Assimp}} is probably not the best choice for loading {\itshape huge} terrains -\/ fully triangulated data takes extremely much free store and should be avoided as long as possible (typically you\textquotesingle{}ll do the triangulation when you actually need to render it). 
\end{DoxyNote}
